Deforestation: What's up with that?

by Nathan Lee

So yesterday I went on an expedition in the Amazon Rainforest to learn about native plants for a new research paper. I was shocked and appalled to see a section on the far south side of the rainforest. After some research, I discovered that about 200 thousand acres of rainforest is cleared each day for agriculture (ironically) and cattle ranching. This is serious. This land could be used to fit 200,000 trees to replace the carbon emissions that humans are creating. Also, most of the clearing is done by big companies like Mitsubishi Motors for various reasons.
